    Worksheet Make Connections - Eigen Review
    

    Objectives

    \begin{enumerate} \item How do eigenvalues relate to the invertibility of a matrix? \end{enumerate}
    🔗
    🔗
    \\
    🔗
    Two convenient Sage tools. If A is a square matrix, then
    🔗
    • A.eigenvalues() gives a list of the eigenvalues, and
      🔗
      🔗
    • (A - lambda*identity\_matrix(n)).right\_kernel() gives a basis of the null space \(\nul(A-\lambda I)\) if \(n\) is the number of rows and columns of \(A\) and \(\lambda\) is an eigenvalue. How does this help you find eigenvectors?
      🔗
      🔗

    1.

    Consider the matrix \(A = \begin{bmatrix} -2 \amp 8 \\ 2 \amp -2 \\ \end{bmatrix}\text{.}\)
    🔗

    (a)

    Find the eigenvalues of \(A\) and a basis for each of the eigenspaces.
    🔗
    🔗

    (b)

    Is \(A\) diagonalizable? If so, find \(P\) and \(D\) and verify that \(A = PDP^{-1}\text{.}\)
    🔗
    🔗

    (c)

    Find a diagonalization of \(A^3\text{.}\)
    🔗
    🔗

    (d)

    Find a diagonalization of \(A^{-1}\text{.}\)
    🔗
    🔗

    (e)

    Find \(\det(A)\) and explain why \(\det(A)\) equals the product of the eigenvalues of \(A\text{.}\)
    🔗
    🔗
    🔗

    2.

    We know that the eigenvalues of \(A\) are the roots of the characteristic equation \(\det(A-\lambda I) = 0\text{.}\)
    🔗

    (a)

    If \(\lambda = 0\) is an eigenvalue, what does this say about \(\det(A)\text{?}\)
    🔗
    🔗

    (b)

    What does this say about the invertibility of \(A\text{?}\)
    🔗
    🔗

    (c)

    How do the eigenvalues of a matrix tell us about its invertibility?
    🔗
    🔗
    🔗

    3.

    Last class we looked at the transition matrix \(A=\left[\begin{array}{cc}0.8\amp0.6\\0.2\amp0.4\end{array}\right]\) applied to trucks at two locations. We wanted to know the long-term behavior of an initial state \(\xvec_0\) that evolves as \(\xvec_{k+1}=A\xvec_k\text{.}\) In other words, what happened to \(A^k\xvec_0\) as \(k\) grows large. We found that \(A\) had eigenvalues \(1\) and \(0.2\text{,}\) and the \(\lim_{k\rightarrow\infty} A^k = \left[\begin{array}{cc}0.75\amp0.75\\0.25\amp0.25\end{array}\right]\text{.}\) So if we start with 1000 trucks total so that \(P+Q=1000\text{,}\) then in the long-term we have
    \begin{equation*} \lim_{k\rightarrow\infty} A^k = \left[\begin{array}{cc}0.75\amp0.75\\0.25\amp0.25\end{array}\right]\twovec{P}{Q} = P\twovec{0.75}{0.25}+Q\twovec{0.75}{0.25} = 1000\twovec{0.75}{0.25} =\twovec{750}{250}, \end{equation*}
    so there will be 750 trucks at location \(P\) and 250 at location \(Q\text{.}\)
    🔗
    Let’s look at a different transition matrix: \(B = \begin{bmatrix} 0 \amp 1 \\ 1 \amp 0 \\ \end{bmatrix}\text{.}\)
    🔗

    (a)

    Find the eigenvalues of \(B\) and bases for its eigenspaces.
    🔗
    🔗

    (b)

    Find a diagonalization of \(B\text{.}\)
    🔗
    🔗

    (c)

    What happens when you look at \(B^k\) as \(k\) grows?
    🔗
    🔗

    (d)

    What is different about the eigenvalues of \(A\) and \(B\) that distinguishes the long-term behavior?
    🔗
