    Worksheet Section 2.3 - The Span of a Set of Vectors
    

    Objectives

    • What is the span of a set of vectors and how do we denote it?
      🔗
      🔗
    • How can we determine if a vector is in the span of another set of vectors?
      🔗
      🔗
    • How do we know if the span of a set of vectors \(\vvec_1, \vvec_2, \dots, \vvec_n\) is \(\mathbb{R}^m\) using the pivots of the matrix \([\vvec_1 \vvec_2 \vvec_3 \dots \vvec_n]\text{?}\)
      🔗
      🔗
    🔗

    1.

    Let’s start in 2-dimensions, and suppose that
    \begin{equation*} \vvec_1=\twovec11,~~~ \vvec_2=\twovec{-3}{-3},~~~ \vvec_3=\twovec14. \end{equation*}
    🔗

    (a)

    Is \(\twovec66\) in the span of \(\vvec_1\) and \(\vvec_2\text{?}\)
    🔗
    🔗

    (b)

    Is \(\twovec45\) in the span of \(\vvec_1\) and \(\vvec_2\text{?}\)
    🔗
    🔗

    (c)

    Give a geometric description of the span of \(\vvec_1\) and \(\vvec_2\text{,}\) denoted \(\span{\vvec_1,\vvec_2}\text{.}\)
    🔗
    🔗

    (d)

    Can \(\twovec45\) be expressed as a linear combination of \(\vvec_1\) and \(\vvec_3\text{?}\) Is \(\twovec45\) in the span of \(\vvec_1\) and \(\vvec_3\text{?}\)
    🔗
    🔗

    (e)

    Give a geometric description of the span of \(\vvec_1\) and \(\vvec_3\text{.}\)
    🔗
    🔗

    (f)

    For what vectors \(\bvec\) does the equation \ \ \ \ \(\begin{bmatrix} 1 \amp 1 \\ 1 \amp 4 \\ \end{bmatrix} \xvec = \bvec\) \ \ \ \ have a solution? Explain your thinking.
    🔗
    🔗
    🔗

    2.

    Now we’ll look at the span of sets of vectors in \(\real^3\text{.}\)
    🔗

    (a)

    Consider the vector \(\vvec =\threevec121\text{.}\) Give a geometric description of \(\laspan\{\vvec\}\) and a rough sketch of the span below.
    🔗
    \includegraphics[scale=.9]{coordinates-3d}
    🔗
    🔗

    (b)

    Now consider the vectors
    \begin{equation*} \evec_1 = \threevec100,~~~ \evec_2 = \threevec010. \end{equation*}
    Give a geometric description of \(\laspan\{\evec_1,\evec_2\}\) and a rough sketch of the span below.
    🔗
    \includegraphics[scale=.9]{coordinates-3d}
    🔗
    🔗

    (c)

    Let’s look at this situation algebraically. If we write \(\bvec=\threevec{b_1}{b_2}{b_3}\text{,}\) what condition on the components of \(\bvec\) must be satisfied if the equation
    \begin{equation*} \begin{bmatrix} 1 \amp 0 \\ 0 \amp 1 \\ 0 \amp 0 \\ \end{bmatrix} \xvec = \bvec \end{equation*}
    is consistent? What condition on the components of \(\bvec\) must be satisfied for \(\bvec\) to be in the span of \(\evec_1\) and \(\evec_2\text{?}\)
    🔗
    🔗
    🔗

    3.

    Consider the vectors
    \begin{equation*} \vvec_1 = \threevec11{-1},~~~ \vvec_2 = \threevec021,~~~ \vvec_3 = \threevec1{-2}4. \end{equation*}
    🔗

    (a)

    Is the vector \(\threevec4{-3}{-1}\) in \(\laspan\{\vvec_1,\vvec_2,\vvec_3\}\text{?}\)
    🔗
    🔗

    (b)

    What is the reduced row echelon form of the matrix \(\begin{bmatrix} \vvec_1 \amp \vvec_2 \amp \vvec_3 \end{bmatrix} \text{?}\) You should be able to find this using your work from the last question.
    🔗
    🔗

    (c)

    What do the pivot positions of this matrix tell you about \(\laspan\{\vvec_1,\vvec_2,\vvec_3\}\text{?}\) Give a geometric description of \(\laspan\{\vvec_1,\vvec_2,\vvec_3\}\text{.}\)
